About Me

I am a PhD student in Thayer Engineering at Dartmouth College, advised by Prof. Bryce L. Ferguson. My research focuses on optimization and decision-making under uncertainty, with particular interest in information design, game theory, Bayesian persuasion, Bayesian congestion games, and multi-agent systems.

Before Dartmouth, I received my M.S. in Applied Statistics from the University of Michigan. I collaborated with Prof. Max Z. Li on air traffic management and geospatial network optimization, with Prof. Donglin Zeng on longitudinal health data analysis and behavioral statistics, and with Prof. Yuekai Sun on causal inference and synthetic control methods. I also hold a B.Sc. in Statistics from Zhongnan University of Economics and Law.

Information design and multi-agent systems. I study how planners can design recommendations that remain incentive-compatible when users may hold heterogeneous beliefs.
Optimization for transportation systems. I work on safe and scalable optimization methods for congestion, air traffic management, and multi-agent coordination.
Statistical modeling for networked and health systems. My previous work includes mixed-effects modeling, longitudinal data analysis, and synthetic control methods.

Selected Publications

Robust Information Design with Heterogeneous Beliefs in Bayesian Congestion Games CDC 2026 Submission
Paper
Yuwei Hu, Bryce L. Ferguson
Developed a robust information-design framework for Bayesian congestion games with heterogeneous user beliefs. Characterized feasibility of robust obedience constraints, derived computable robustness certificates, and analyzed the value impact of stronger robustness requirements.
Information Design · Bayesian Congestion Games · Heterogeneous Beliefs · Game Theory · Bayesian Persuasion
Provably Safe Optimization of Arrival Flows into Terminal Airspace AIAA AVIATION Forum 2026
Code
Serra Dane, Kuang Sun, Yuwei Hu, Eric Boccaccio, Adrian Nomi, Joshua Glottmann, Jean-Baptiste Jeannin, Max Z. Li
Developed a provably safe optimization framework for sequencing and scheduling arrival flows in terminal airspace. Formulated the problem under safety-critical dynamic constraints and demonstrated scalable multi-agent coordination in high-density traffic scenarios.
Safe Optimization · Air Traffic Management · Multi-Agent Systems · Constrained Control · Robust Scheduling
Temporal Variation in Psychosocial Factors and Physical Activity Levels Among Patients With Heart Failure Circulation: Heart Failure 2025
